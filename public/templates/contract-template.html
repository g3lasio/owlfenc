Ultimate Contractor Protections
A. Before Work Begins
  • Contractor may conduct a pre-construction site survey; hidden issues, code violations, or hazardous conditions may trigger change orders and extra fees.
  • Work will not start until all permits and written client approvals are provided. Delays in permits or client documentation are at client’s risk and cost.
  • Project start date is flexible if delayed by client or by force majeure; contractor may revise schedule and pricing at their sole discretion.
B. During Work
  • Client must keep site accessible and safe; failure to do so authorizes the contractor to suspend work and bill additional costs.
  • Any additional requests, upgrades, or changes from the client require a signed change order and payment for extra labor/materials before proceeding.
  • If hazardous material is found, work halts immediately; abatement is client’s responsibility and billed separately. Contractor is held harmless for delays or extra costs due to hazardous conditions.
  • Contractor is not responsible for delays caused by weather, third-party vendors, supply chain shortages, government mandates, or events outside their control.
  • If client or others damage installed work, contractor will repair at client’s expense.
C. Upon and After Completion
  • Upon completion, contractor notifies client. Client must inspect and approve within five (5) business days, or work is deemed accepted and final payment is due.
  • If client finds legitimate defects, contractor must be given the opportunity to repair. No payment may be withheld for cosmetic or non-contractual issues.
  • If final payment is delayed, contractor may place a lien, stop all warranty, or initiate collection/legal action with full recovery of fees and interest.
  • Warranty is void if the client or third parties alter, damage, or misuse the work, or if payment is not made in full.
